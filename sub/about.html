About CGIS

CGIS Introduction

CGIS is a system for the management of greenhouse gas emissions
from Cagayan Valley in Philippines and the dissemination of relevant
information to the general public.
As the occurrence of natural disasters such as typhoons, floods and heat waves in the Cagayan
Valley increases recently, the importance of the reduction of greenhouse gases also increases.
We hope for the establishing of goals for the reduction of regional GHG using information
provided through CGIS as well as encouraging interest and participation in this matter.

Overall management of the regional
GHG inventory

  • Building up the inventory and calculating
    the emissions by region and by sector
  • Data management
  • Resources for setting up GHG reduction plans

Public information service

  • About GHG (definition and effect)
  • Inventory statistical information (province/sector)
  • References including inventory reports

International coordination
scheme for the setting-up of CGIS

CGIS, a pilot project of 2018 Seoul Initiative Network, was set-up at the
request of NEDA in Cagayan Valley.
Seoul Initiative Network Business is a UNESCAP project with the support of the Korean government
which aims to support the sustainable growth of the Members of the Asia Pacific Region.
For the details and examples of the business, please visit the website (www.singg.or.kr).
